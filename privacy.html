Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 23, 2026

The short version: QuietCase does not collect, transmit, or store any of your data on external servers. Everything you log stays on your device. We have no analytics, no tracking, no accounts, and no way to see your data. Period.

1. Who we are

QuietCase ("we", "us", "our") is a mobile application published on the Apple App Store. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use the QuietCase app ("the App").

2. Information we collect

We collect nothing.

QuietCase is designed to operate entirely on your device. We do not collect, receive, transmit, or have access to any personal information, usage data, or content you create within the App. Specifically:

  • We do not require an account, login, or registration of any kind.
  • We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any other personal identifier.
  • We do not collect device identifiers, IP addresses, or advertising IDs.
  • We do not use any analytics frameworks, crash reporting services, or tracking SDKs.
  • We do not use cookies or similar tracking technologies.
  • The App does not make any network requests. It operates fully offline.

3. Data stored on your device

QuietCase stores the following data locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework:

  • Noise incident logs: Timestamps, decibel readings, noise type, severity ratings, location direction, and free-text descriptions you enter.
  • Audio recordings: Short audio clips (if you choose to record them) saved as .m4a files in the App's sandboxed storage.
  • Your profile information: Name, address, unit number, and landlord/manager details you optionally enter for use in PDF reports.
  • App preferences: Quiet hours settings, recording quality preferences, and other configuration choices.

This data exists only on your device (and in your personal iCloud backup if you have device backups enabled through Apple). We cannot access, view, or retrieve this data under any circumstances.

4. Microphone usage

QuietCase requests permission to access your device's microphone for two purposes:

  • Decibel measurement: The App uses the microphone to measure ambient sound levels in real time. This audio data is processed on-device to calculate decibel readings and is not recorded or stored unless you explicitly choose to record an audio clip.
  • Audio evidence recording: If you tap the "Record" button, the App will record a short audio clip (up to 60 seconds) and save it locally to your device. This recording is never transmitted anywhere.

You may revoke microphone access at any time through your device's Settings app. Without microphone access, the decibel measurement and audio recording features will be unavailable, but you can still manually log incidents.

5. PDF reports

When you generate a PDF report, it is created entirely on your device. The report may contain the incident data and personal information you have entered into the App. When you share a report using the iOS share sheet, it is sent through whatever channel you choose (email, AirDrop, Messages, etc.). We have no involvement in or visibility into this sharing.

6. Third-party services

QuietCase uses zero third-party services. No analytics (no Google Analytics, Firebase, Mixpanel, or similar). No advertising networks. No crash reporting tools. No social media SDKs. The App is entirely self-contained.

7. Children's privacy

QuietCase does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Since the App collects no data whatsoever, there is no risk of inadvertent collection of children's information.

8. Data deletion

Since all data resides on your device, you have complete control over it:

  • You can delete individual incidents from within the App.
  • You can clear all data using the "Clear All Data" option in Settings.
  • You can delete the App entirely, which removes all associated data from your device.

We cannot delete your data because we do not have it.

9. Data security

Your data is protected by the security measures built into iOS, including the device passcode, Face ID/Touch ID, and Apple's app sandboxing. Data stored by QuietCase is accessible only to the App itself and is not shared with other apps on your device. Audio recordings and incident logs are stored within the App's sandboxed container, which is encrypted at rest when your device is locked.

10. Changes to this policy

If we ever change this Privacy Policy, we will update this page and the effective date above. If a future version of the App were to collect any data (which we have no plans to do), we would notify you through an in-app notice before any collection begins and would require your explicit consent.
